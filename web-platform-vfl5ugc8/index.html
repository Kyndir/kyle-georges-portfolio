Case Studies

A closer look at how the work got done.

01

Content Systems & Automation

Replacing manual busywork with a repeatable content system.

Problem

Content scheduling, file organization, and reporting were handled manually across scattered folders and spreadsheets, consuming hours every week that could go toward creative work.

Goal

Design a repeatable system that automates the administrative side of content production without limiting creative flexibility.

My Role

Founder & Creative Director - owned strategy, workflow design, and implementation end to end.

Technologies

Google Drive AutomationCanvaGoogle Analytics 4

Process

Audited the existing workflow to find repeated manual steps, mapped out a folder and naming convention that could scale across clients, then built templated automations around that structure.

Challenges

Balancing rigid automation with the need for creative flexibility on a per-client basis was the main tension - overly strict systems slowed the team down instead of freeing them up.

Solution

Built modular automation templates with clear override points, so the system handled the repetitive parts while creative decisions stayed flexible.

Measurable Outcomes

+Add: hours saved per week on admin tasks
+Add: reduction in content turnaround time
+Add: number of clients supported by the system

Lessons Learned

Automation works best when it removes friction rather than adding rules - the goal is protecting creative time, not replacing creative judgment.

02

Video Production Pipeline

A standardized workflow for consistent, professional social video.

Problem

Video quality and turnaround time varied from project to project, making it hard to plan client content calendars with confidence.

Goal

Create a dependable shoot-to-edit pipeline that delivers cinematic, on-brand video within tight social media turnaround windows.

My Role

Founder & Creative Director - shooter, editor, and process owner.

Technologies

DJI Ronin-SC2Canva

Process

Standardized pre-production with shot lists and framing guides, refined gimbal shooting technique for consistent motion, and built templated export presets for each platform format.

Challenges

Tight deadlines and multiple format requirements - stories, reels, and ads - meant footage had to be reusable across very different aspect ratios and lengths.

Solution

Shot with multi-format reuse in mind from the start and used templated presets so editing time went into polish rather than repetitive setup.

Measurable Outcomes

+Add: average turnaround time per video
+Add: engagement lift on video content
+Add: number of videos produced per month

Lessons Learned

Planning for reuse during the shoot saves far more time in editing than trying to fix format problems after the fact.

03

AI-Assisted Content Workflows

Using AI as an amplifier for creative work, not a replacement for it.

Problem

Ideation and first-draft writing for client content calendars took up creative hours that could be spent refining ideas instead of generating them from scratch.

Goal

Integrate AI tools responsibly into the content workflow to speed up drafting while keeping every brand's voice authentic and consistent.

My Role

Founder & Creative Director - designed the workflow and prompt approach, reviewed and edited every output.

Technologies

Claude API (Anthropic)Google Analytics 4

Process

Built brand voice guidelines for each client, developed reusable prompt templates around them, and layered in a human review step before anything shipped.

Challenges

Generic AI output was the biggest risk - without careful prompting, drafts felt the same across different brands.

Solution

Paired detailed brand-voice prompts with a consistent editing checklist so AI-assisted drafts still sounded distinctly on-brand.

Measurable Outcomes

+Add: reduction in first-draft time
+Add: increase in content output volume
+Add: client satisfaction or approval rate

Lessons Learned

AI speeds up the blank page problem, but brand voice and final judgment still have to come from a human who knows the client.
